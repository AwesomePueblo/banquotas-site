The Field Guide to Human Perception

The complete BanQuotas Senses Project, compiled into one beautifully formatted, print-ready book—yours to keep, yours to print, yours to return to.

You live inside a thin, bright sliver of the possible. Your eyes drink a single octave of color from a spectrum that runs for miles. Your tongue knows five tastes out of a chemical universe. And at the smallest scales, reality refuses to settle into anything solid until something looks.

The Field Guide to Human Perception gathers the three signature BanQuotas journeys into a single volume—edited, sequenced, and typeset as a finished book with a cover, an original introduction, and a clean design built for reading and for printing. It is the same poetic-scientific voice you know from the site, given the permanence of a page you can hold.

What's inside

Chapter I — Light

A soul rides the entire electromagnetic spectrum, from the slow dark swell of radio waves through the narrow miracle of visible color to the annihilating fire of gamma rays.

Chapter II — Taste

A descent onto the warm landscape of the tongue—sweet, salty, sour, bitter, umami—and a wandering out past the edge of everything a human can taste.

Chapter III — Quantum Perception

Light at its smallest scale, where a photon declines to be wave or particle until we ask, and where observing seems to reach backward into the past.

Plus an original introduction written for this edition, a table of contents, and a clean print-ready design. Roughly 6,000 words across three chapters—a single self-contained HTML book you open in any browser and read, or print to a keepsake PDF.
